Student Learning Outcomes:

We expect all students who complete math classes todemonstrate the ability to:
  • Understand and utilize the essential course content at an appropriate leve.
  • Use problem-solving skills, such as developing a strategic overview of a mathematical situation and using this to analyze that situation.
  • Recognize that a problem can have different useful representations (graphical, numerical, or symbolic) and select the most appropriate method and format.
  • Model real world problems mathematically and interpret the results appropriately.
  • Use appropriate software and technological tools and judge when such use is helpful.
  • Communicate mathematical results and arguments clearly, both orally and in writing.
  • Appreciate the central role of mathematics in the sciences and the real world.

Course Objectives:

By the end of Math 118 students should be able to:
  • interpret the slope, intercepts, or any given point on a model with regard to the application
  • Determine what is a function
  • Evaluate functions represented by equations, tables, and graphs
  • Solve equations involving linear, quadratic, rational, radical, literal, exponential, and logarithmic functions as well as functions defined by a table or graph
  • State the domain of polynomial, rational, radical, exponential, and logarithmic functions as well as functions defined by graphs, tebles, a composition, or as an inverse
  • Make predictions using a model and interpret the results
  • Find a model from words, state a reasonable domain with regard to the application, and use a calculator to find the extrema of the model
  • Recognize the characteristics of the graph of power, absolute value, exponential, logarithmic and piece-wise defined functions
  • Successfully use logarithm and exponent rules
  • Model exponential and power functions
  • Recognize characteristics of graphs of trigonometric, inverse trigonometric and right triangles.
  • Use trigonometric identities to evaluate trigonometric functions and solve trigonometric equations
