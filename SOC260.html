SOC 260

The Family in Society

Instructor: Rick Bulcroft, PhD
Email: richard.bulcroft@wwu.edu
Office hours: TW: 1:00-2:00 Onlinel or Bu Appointment
Preferred pronouns: He / Him
Location and Time: online; Asynchronous

Method of Evaluation:

QUIZZES 20%
MID-TERM EXAMS 30%
FINAL EXAM 30%
Small Group Activites 20%



    Modules:

  1. Sociological Perspectives on Families
  2. Modern American Families - Origins and Institutional Linkages
  3. Review and Assessment - 1
  4. Sociological Perspectives - Classical (MACRO)
  5. Sociological Perspectives - Contemporary (MICRO)
  6. Review and Assessment - 2
  7. Family Diversity
  8. Pre-Marital and Non-Marital Relationships
  9. Marital Relationships
  10. Parenting and Parenthood
  11. Review and Assessment - 3

The purpose of this course is three-fold: (1) To provide you with an overview of theoretical and methodological ideas and issues that are preparatory for more advanced courses in Sociology; (2) To provide you with an introduction to the field of Family Sociology; and (3) To give you an opportunity to critically and soundly examine the role of the family in today's society.
